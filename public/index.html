Overview

No-regret learning in games sits at the intersection of game theory and online learning. This tutorial focuses on the framework of \(\Phi\)-regret and its close connection to \(\Phi\)-equilibria. The framework measures a learner against a chosen class of strategy deviations \(\Phi\), interpolating between external regret, swap regret, and richer notions of hindsight rationality.

The goal is to give a unified algorithmic view of recent advances in learning and computation of \(\Phi\)-equilibria, especially for complex and high-dimensional strategy spaces where classical swap regret is too expensive and external regret is too weak.

The tutorial begins with the foundational Gordon-Greenwald-Marks reduction and then turns to newer tools: semi-separation, expected fixed points, multicalibration, and TreeSwap-style reductions for large action spaces.
